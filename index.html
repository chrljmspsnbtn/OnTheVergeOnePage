About Us

We do not believe
in wasted people.

On The Verge is a youth-led social enterprise built on one conviction: that unrealized potential is not a personal failure — it is a systemic one, and a solvable one.


We intervene early — in schools, in communities, and in the spaces where potential is most at risk of being abandoned. Through belief-based development, mindset intervention, and research-grounded tools, we meet people at the exact moment they are on the verge of giving up — and walk with them across it.

Turning suppressed potential into lived reality.

On The Verge exists to meet people at the edge of what they almost didn't try — and help them cross it. Through early intervention, belief-based development, and the courage to take the first step before being ready, we turn suppressed potential into lived reality.

Our Story

How it all began.

On The Verge did not begin with a business plan. It began with a question: why do so many people live so far below what they are clearly capable of? Not because of lack of talent. Not because of lack of desire. But because of something quieter and more insidious — the accumulated weight of fear, culture, and a world that teaches people to wait until they are ready.

In early 2026, what started as personal research became something more structured. Three problem claims emerged from the evidence: that ideas are dismantled before they are ever tried; that people consistently find more reasons to fail than to succeed; and that failure itself — rather than being embraced as curriculum — is treated as a verdict that ends the story.

Against each problem, three solution claims were identified: that belief is the first step in any meaningful achievement; that the brain cannot distinguish imagination from reality, making visualization a genuine neurological tool; and that trying before you are ready is always better than waiting for perfect conditions that never arrive.

From that foundation, On The Verge was formally established — a youth-led social enterprise built to do what no one else was doing in quite this way: intervene early, intervene personally, and refuse to accept that the gap between a person and their potential is permanent.

Our first act was research. We designed a survey — The Unrealized Potential Survey — to gather real data from real people: students, graduates, office workers, educators, and parents, all asked to reflect honestly on fear, ambition, creativity, and the forces that shaped their choices. That survey is currently ongoing. The data it returns will become the evidence base for everything we build next.

We are early. We are building in real time. And we are doing it on purpose — because the whole point of On The Verge is to try before you are ready.
