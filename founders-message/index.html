Founder’s Message

A brief message from the Founder.

Why On The Verge exists, and why this mission matters.

In an age where instant gratification is the norm, failure is permanence, ideas are discarded in favor of more “realistic” and “practical” approaches, dreams and ambitions die every day due to fear of failure, and life itself does not always seem to work for us, there is a need for individuals to go beyond their comfort zone and be On The Verge of beginning something new that results in human fulfilment.

On The Verge began when I reflected on my life’s achievements and my search for purpose. As an active student leader, foreign exchange student, intercultural learning advocate, and self-improvement advocate, I have learned a great deal that has helped me not only in academics, but also in how I handle the affairs of my life.

At a specific point in time in 2026, I began to ask myself: what I have is special, and what I have can be shared with people. But I then realized that who I am today was not who I was a few years ago, and all it took was a change in belief and understanding of my environment to change my life ten-fold.

Being raised in a Filipino household, limitations, restrictions, and do’s or don’ts have always been commonplace. However, I was fortunate enough to have parents who were open to my exploration of the world and my ambitions, albeit with an emphasis on my intellectual growth and education.

I grew up in a small town with a small community, and I always struggled to fit in and make my mark because everything had already been set up. Those who were considered smart were the ones being sent forward, while those who had potential were often treated as extras.

Everything changed when I moved to a city and to a bigger school, where doors of opportunity opened up and welcomed me. It made me realize that the environment we live in, the perspective we believe in, and the limitations we place on ourselves are all shaped by our own actions and choices.

It is this exact experience that convicted me to create On The Verge. To borrow a quote from the philosopher Seneca, “We suffer more often in imagination than in reality.” Self-imposed limitation, disempowering belief, negativity bias, and fear of failure all contribute to this.

The worst part is that it is silent, and we do not acknowledge or recognize it enough. This leads to dreams unfulfilled, ideas untried, ambitions abandoned, and lives settled in permanent stagnation.

You visited us for a reason, and that in itself shows that you want a more fulfilling and meaningful life. Join us, and let us push you on the verge of your potential.

— Charlie James P. Batin, Founder
